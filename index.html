SyncTalklip

Abstract

"Talking face generation" (TFG) has high requirements for accurate, natural, and synchronized videos, and taking into account each specific is a great challenge. In this paper, we propose SyncTalklip, a novel network framework that aims to address the challenges of synchronization and lip-reading performance in TFG tasks. By deeply analyzing the interaction mechanism between visual and auditory information, our approach not only ensures tight synchronization between the input speech and the generated visual content, but also accurately reflects the true meaning of the linguistic content. We introduce SyncAvhubert, a model that combines the advantages of semantic-level alignment and temporal alignment, which effectively avoids the problem of subtle lip synchronization by bringing audio and video representations closer together in an abstract distance space while enhancing the personalized features of the generated images. Experimental results show that SyncTalklip outperforms existing methods on several evaluation metrics, demonstrating its superiority in generating accurate, natural, and synchronized talking heads. In addition, our study emphasizes the importance of considering how audiovisual information integrally affects audience perception and understanding during TFG content creation or evaluation.Experiments demonstrates that our approach outperforms existing state-of-the-art (SOTA) techniques.
