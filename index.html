SpeechBind: Connecting All Co-Speech Modalities

with Self-Supervised Speech Discrete Units

Abstract

Multimodal communication involves intricate interactions across various modalities, necessitating a profound understanding not only of audio speech but also of co-speech modal data such as talking head movements, co-speech gestures, subtitles, etc., to gain comprehensive insights. However, the scarcity of co-speech modality corpora hampers research in this area. In this paper, we introduce \textsc{SpeechBind} to seamlessly integrate all co-speech modalities with speech discrete units. These units are derived from SSL models pre-trained on a large number of audio speech and can comprehensively encompass most of the information present in (co-)speech modality. By employing them as a guide, we achieve faster, more reliable, and data-efficient co-speech representation learning. Furthermore, we leverage discrete units as a medium to achieve zero-shot alignment between any (co-)speech modalities, enhancing speech robustness for tasks lacking paired data. Finally, we explore the transfer of speech knowledge to co-speech modalities, including the transfer of cross-language mapping knowledge from audio speech to talking heads. Experiments demonstrate that \textsc{SpeechBind} effectively binds all (co-)speech modalities using discrete units, unlocking numerous novel capabilities.
